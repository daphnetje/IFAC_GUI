For this decision task, we believe that white men are more frequently associated with high incomes, while other demographic groups are more often linked to low incomes. Our goal is to uncover which white men may be benefiting from favoritism and which other groups may be experiencing discrimination.

üîç How it works:

  • Defining groups: We identify at-risk groups based on a mix of sensitive characteristics (e.g., sex, race) and neutral characteristics (e.g., occupation, education level). Each group is linked to a decision outcome (e.g., income = low or high).
  • Confidence: This measures how strongly a decision outcome is associated with a group. A higher confidence means that a large proportion of people in the group experience the same outcome.
  • Slift: We compare the confidence of a decision outcome for a group with the same group after swapping the sensitive characteristics:
    • If the original group consists of white men, we check how often the outcome applies to non-white men.
    • If the original group consists of any other demographic group (e.g., women, white women, Black people, Black women, Black men), we compare it to the reference group of white men.
    • A large shift suggests that the sensitive characteristic may be influencing decisions unfairly.
